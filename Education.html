DISSERTATION

Ph.D. Dissertation Title “Nonlinear Multi-Filed Coupling Characteristics of Micro-/Nano-Scale Functional Devices”. Supervisor: Prof. Xi Wang (xwang@sjtu.edu.cn)

Conducted theoretical analysis of nonlinear electromechanical coupling of micro-/nano-devices considering quantum and thermal fluctuation-induced attraction. First proposed unifying nonlocal stress and strain gradient model to investigate size-dependent pull-in instability of nano sensors and actuators. Studied surface and small scale effects on resonant frequency and free-standing behaviors of nano-devices. Numerically analyzed nonlinear dynamic response and bifurcation of electrostatically actuated devices.

Master’s Dissertation Title “Acoustic Properties of Natural Fiber Reinforced Composites”. Supervisor: Prof. Yan Li (liyan@tongji.edu.cn), co-supervisor: Prof. Yongdong Pan (ypan@tongji.edu.cn)

Optimized interfacial properties of layered composites by fiber surface treatment with coupling agent. Experimentally and theoretically analyzed the sound absorption of high frequency band of composite laminates and sandwiches by impedance tube based on transfer function method. Investigated effects of multiscale structure composition of natural fibers on sound energy dissipation by SEM and TEM.

Bachelor’s Dissertation Title “Moisture Absorption and Flame Retardancy of Natural Fiber Reinforced Composites”. Supervisor: Prof. Yan Li (liyan@tongji.edu.cn)

Developed natural fiber reinforced composites with good moisture resistance and flame retardancy properties by fiber surface treatment and montmorillonite (MMT) nanoparticles intercalation.

科研兴趣

微/纳机电系统(MEMS/NEMS)借助于飞速发展的微纳米加工技术，并融合力、热、电、磁、光等多物理场在微纳米尺度下的新现象与新机理，在多个学科领域体现出了极为广阔的应用前景。近年来，已有许多类别的微/纳米层级功能化器件被广泛应用于微芯片、柔性电子、细胞与DNA分子检测、药物传递以及量子传输等重要领域。因此，微纳米器件的基础理论与原理研究工作就显得越发地重要与迫切，只有对纳米材料与结构器件的新现象及其机理的深入理解，具有复杂结构且功能优化的新型微纳米器件才会不断地被设计与制作出来，从某种程度上讲，纳米科学与纳米技术将引领人类步入新的历史发展阶段。

本课题希望通过考虑表面能与非局部应力-应变梯度的统一理论来对基于纳米复合材料及结构的功能化电子器件进行理论建模，并采用同伦分析方法获得多场耦合下强非线性微分控制方程的解析解，并用微分求积法数值分析表面弹性、表面张力以及小尺度效应对非线性解的影响，同时，研究van der Waals力以及量子波动与热涨落引起的Casimir效应对微纳米器件静态与动态结构稳定性的影响，分析微纳米功能器件在电场、温度场、磁场、非线性、静电/交变电场驱动、电场边缘效应、器件结构参数等因素影响下的工作性能；另外，建立具有多层复合结构微纳米器件的理论模型，并结合有限元模拟或分子动力学方法，研究基于纳米复合材料的电子器件在纳尺度下力-电-热-磁多场耦合作用下的相关静力学与动力学行为，研究成果有望对具有复杂结构微纳米器件的功能化设计提供理论参考。
